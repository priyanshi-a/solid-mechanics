Maxwell's reciprocal theorem on beams

Theory

Maxwell's reciprocal theorem :

Maxwell says that the deflection at a point A due to a unit load at point B is the same as the deflection at B if a unit load were applied at A.

according to Maxwell's reciprocal theorem, from the above diagrams, D1 = D2.

              A beam extremely supported at both ends and load applied normal to axis of beam is called simply supported beam. The deflection of a beam are an engineering concern as they can create an unstable structure if they are large. People do not want to work in a building in which the floor beams deflective an excessive amount, even though it may be in no danger of failing. Consequently limits are often placed upon the allowable deflections of a beam, as well as upon the stresses.

              When the loads are applied to a beam their originally straight axes become curved. Displacements from the initial axes are called bending or flexural deflections.
